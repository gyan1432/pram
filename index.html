Welcome to Explore India

Discover the beauty and diversity of India's most iconic destinations.

Mumbai

Gateway of India

The Gateway of India is an arch-monument completed in 1924 on the waterfront of Mumbai (Bombay), India. It was erected to commemorate the landing of George V for his coronation as the Emperor of India in December 1911 at Strand Road near Wellington Fountain.

Marine Drive

Marine Drive is a 3.6 km long boulevard in South Mumbai. It is known for its stunning views of the Arabian Sea and is a popular spot for watching the sunset.

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus

This historic railway station, formerly known as Victoria Terminus, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is an outstanding example of Victorian Gothic Revival architecture in India.

Elephanta Caves

Located on Elephanta Island, these rock-cut temples are dedicated to the Hindu god Shiva. The caves date back to the 5th to 8th centuries and are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Explore Uttar Pradesh

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is one of the most famous landmarks in the world. This white marble mausoleum in Agra was built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Varanasi Ghats

Varanasi, one of the oldest cities in the world, is renowned for its ghats along the Ganges River. These ghats are significant in Hinduism and attract pilgrims and tourists from around the world.

Fatehpur Sikri

Fatehpur Sikri, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, was the capital of the Mughal Empire under Akbar's reign. It is known for its stunning architecture and historical significance.

Lucknow Bara Imambara

The Bara Imambara is an architectural marvel in Lucknow, built by Nawab Asaf-ud-Daula in 1784. It is famous for its central hall, which is one of the largest arched constructions without support beams.

Delhi

Red Fort

The Red Fort is a historic fort in Delhi that served as the main residence of the Mughal Emperors. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of India's rich history and culture.

Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar is a towering minaret in Delhi, standing at 73 meters tall. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the finest examples of Indo-Islamic architecture.

India Gate

The India Gate is a war memorial located in New Delhi. It was built in memory of the soldiers who died in World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War. It is a prominent tourist attraction in the city.

Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple, known for its distinctive flower-like shape, is a Bahá'í House of Worship in Delhi. It is open to all faiths and is a symbol of peace and unity.
